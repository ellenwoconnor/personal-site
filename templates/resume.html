Overview: As a linguist-turned-engineer, my expertise lies at the intersection of language, technology and cognitive science, and their applications within both research and industry.

Professional

2016 - Present: Software Engineer - Godaddy (Locu), San Francisco CA

Our app helps small business owners improve their search engine visibility by giving targeted suggestions for improving the text content on a user's personal site, and then automatically applying those changes to their existing site for them. As a full-stack developer I help build our apps and refine them using quantitative product research (a/b testing).

2009 - 2016: Research Fellow - USC (Department of Linguistics), Los Angeles CA

As a graduate research fellow, I investigated information processing strategies in language and other cognitive domains. My psycholinguistic research led me to design and implement dozens of experiments, and I began using Matlab, Python & R to process, clean, analyze and visualize experimental data. During my time at USC I also developed an interest in neuroscience, serving as a research assistant for an fMRI project looking at decision-making networks in the brain, and a visiting scholar at the NYU Neuroscience of Language Lab.

2003 - 2006: Content Developer - Alelo, Los Angeles CA

I first began to flex my linguist muscles at Alelo, where I designed chatbots to help users acquire foreign language proficiency. My role there started with copy writing/curriculum development, but then rapidly expanded into content strategy and product management. As an added bonus, I got to engage in linguistic fieldwork on understudied languages like Dari and Pashto to support speech recognition models and language learning curricula.

2003 - 2006: Writing Tutor - UCSC (Learning Support Services), Santa Cruz CA

During college I was nominated by a UCSC professor to be a writing tutor for incoming freshmen, and spent several years helping students improve their critical thinking, essay organization and English skills.
